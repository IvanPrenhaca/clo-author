# Frontier Map — Pediatric CIF/SBS Prevalence

**Project:** cif-brazil | **Compiled:** 2026-07-13 (librarian)

## What has been done internationally

### 1. Population-based incidence (live-birth denominator)
- **Wales 2004 (Canada):** first true population-based neonatal SBS estimate — 24.5 per 100,000 live births; much higher in premature infants; case-fatality 37.5%.
- **Cole 2008 (US NICHD network):** VLBW (<1500 g) surgical SBS incidence, the source of the "~7 per 1,000 among low-birth-weight" figure.
- **Puttonen 2023 (Finland):** ~24 per 100,000 live births via a national "data lake" (administrative real-world evidence).
These establish incidence but are live-birth-denominated and mostly neonatal.

### 2. National / registry prevalence (HPN or diagnosis proxy)
- **Beath 2011 (UK):** paediatric HPN point prevalence 13.7 per million; strong regional concentration; SBS share of HPN cases 27%→63%.
- **Antunes 2020 (Portugal), Lezo 2022 (international/Italy-led):** cross-sectional CIF prevalence from PN-centre surveys; European cohorts include a larger share of congenital enteropathies / motility disorders.
- **Khokhar 2025 (global targeted review):** modelled prevalence across 61 countries — **0.09–1.67 per 100,000 children** in 2024; US 1.49/100k, UK 1.67/100k; Central/South America mean 0.26/100k, **Brazil (extrapolated) 0.14/100k = 1.4 per million**. Explicitly attributes low Latin-American figures to lack of HPN infrastructure and underreporting.

### 3. Administrative / claims-data prevalence with a procedure/diagnosis proxy (the manuscript's methodological family)
- **Pant 2017 (US, Kids' Inpatient Database):** no unique ICD code → combined a diagnosis code with the **PN procedure code** as an SBS proxy; discharge-level (not patient-level) data; documents race/equity and referral-concentration patterns.
- **Ali 2025 (US, Komodo claims):** ≥2 PN/IV claims + malabsorption dx + GI-surgery evidence, **deduplicated to unique patients** → **9 per million pediatric, 36 per million overall (2021)**. The most current and most directly comparable administrative-data prevalence paper.
- **Medina Coeli 2025 (Brazil, IJPDS):** deterministic matching in de-identified **SIH-SUS** (DOB + municipality + CEP) to reconstruct episodes/individuals — the methods precedent for deduplicating the exact database this manuscript uses.

### 4. Clinical outcomes / rehabilitation cohorts (context, not prevalence)
- **Squires 2012, Gattini 2021, Mutanen 2023, Avitzur 2024 (IIFR):** multinational pediatric IF outcomes — 5-yr enteral autonomy ~37–53%, survival ~88–96%, IFALD 18–24%.
- **Latin America / Brazil:** Rumbo 2024 (RESTORE, 20 LatAm centres), Leite 2025 (3 Brazilian MIRPs, 207 pts), Goldani 2022 (HCPA, 54 pts), Boteon 2024 (Brazilian intestinal transplant), Spolidoro 2021, Gondolesi 2020 (LatAm transplant status). All are single-/multi-centre cohorts, none population-based.

### 5. COVID-19 disruption to CIF care
- **Hilberath 2024 (JPGN):** pandemic disruption of paediatric CIF care and PN supply at a tertiary centre; plus international HPN-during-COVID clinician/patient surveys.

## The gap this paper fills

1. **No national, population-based prevalence estimate of pediatric CIF/SBS has ever been produced for Brazil.** Every Brazilian data point to date is a single- or few-centre rehabilitation cohort (Leite, Goldani, Boteon) or a Latin-America-wide registry that enrolls only referred patients (Rumbo/RESTORE). Khokhar's Brazil number (1.4/million) is a *modelled extrapolation from Switzerland/regional means*, not observed Brazilian data.

2. **No study has used Brazil's SIH-SUS/DATASUS administrative data with a PN-duration proxy + deterministic record linkage to estimate CIF prevalence.** The methodological pieces exist separately — procedure-code-as-proxy (Pant, Ali) and deterministic SIH-SUS linkage (Medina Coeli) — but have not been combined for this disease in this country.

3. **No one has quantified the spatial/administrative "disappearance" of CIF patients in a continental middle-income health system,** nor cross-validated a PN-based estimate against a newly introduced disease-specific procedure code to demonstrate underreporting.

## Where this paper sits

This paper is the **first population-based Brazilian pediatric CIF prevalence estimate**, and one of very few CIF prevalence studies worldwide built from *national administrative hospital data with individual-level record linkage* rather than PN-centre surveys. It extends the Pant/Ali administrative-data lineage to a middle-income, continental setting, uses the Medina-Coeli-type deterministic-linkage method on the same national database, benchmarks against Khokhar's global figures and Ali's US pediatric rate (9/million), contextualizes within the Rumbo/Leite Latin-American clinical literature, and adds a novel underreporting cross-validation (the 2024 CIF procedure code) plus a COVID-19 access-disruption reading supported by Hilberath.

## Scooping-risk assessment
- **No direct scoop found.** No 2024–2026 paper produces a Brazilian national pediatric CIF/SBS prevalence estimate from administrative data.
- **Nearest neighbours:** Ali 2025 (US claims — same method, different country; a *validating precedent*, not a competitor) and Rumbo 2024 (LatAm registry — referred patients only, no population prevalence). Neither pre-empts the Brazilian population estimate.
- **Watch item:** an unverified 2025 *Intestinal Failure* item ("HPN in children with IF: a descriptive analysis from a Brazilian center") appeared in search but could not be accessed (HTTP 403). It reads as another single-centre descriptive cohort, not a national prevalence study — low scoop risk — but the authors should confirm its content.
