# Positioning — Chronic Intestinal Failure in Brazil (cif-brazil)

**Compiled:** 2026-07-13 (librarian). The librarian collects and organizes; the contribution wording below is a *suggestion* for the writer/strategist, not a mandate.

## Suggested contribution statement (one paragraph)

> We provide the first national, population-based prevalence estimate of pediatric Chronic Intestinal Failure in Brazil. In the absence of a unique ICD-10 diagnosis code, we identify probable CIF cases in nine years of SIH-SUS/DATASUS hospital administrative data using prolonged parenteral nutrition (≥60 daily units within a 74-day window) as a clinically grounded proxy, and we resolve the database's lack of a persistent patient identifier through a deterministic record-linkage algorithm (postal code, sex, date of birth). This combines two methods each previously validated only in isolation — a procedure code as a proxy for a code-less diagnosis (as in US claims/administrative analyses) and deterministic linkage of de-identified SIH-SUS records — and applies them, for the first time, to estimate the burden of a rare pediatric condition in a continental middle-income country. Beyond the headline prevalence (mean 3.35 per million, 2017–2025), we quantify pronounced geographic inequity in access to specialized care, document an incompletely-recovered COVID-19 disruption, and cross-validate against a disease-specific procedure code introduced in 2024 to demonstrate an order-of-magnitude underreporting. Our estimate should be read as a lower bound of the true national burden.

## Which NEW citations most strengthen the INTRODUCTION

1. **`ali2025epidemiology`** — establishes that administrative/claims data with a PN proxy is a legitimate, current way to estimate pediatric SBS-IF prevalence, and gives the cleanest pediatric benchmark (9/million US). Sets up both the method and the comparison.
2. **`rumbo2024restore`** — the definitive statement that Latin-American IF data are scarce and registry-based; motivates "why Brazil needs a population estimate." Replaces the weak `nut2023115` abstract.
3. **`medinacoeli2025deterministic`** — signals early that the deterministic-linkage method is established on SIH-SUS, pre-empting the "can you even deduplicate this database?" objection.
4. **Elevate `pant2017epidemiology`** (already in bib) from the Discussion into the Introduction/Methods as the direct precedent for "procedure code as proxy when no diagnosis code exists."

## Which NEW citations most strengthen the DISCUSSION

1. **`ali2025epidemiology`** (again) — the apples-to-apples benchmark: 9/million (US pediatric) vs 3.35/million (Brazil) frames the underdiagnosis argument quantitatively.
2. **`hilberath2024impact`** — the missing citation for the COVID-19 access-disruption interpretation of the 2020–2021 dip; currently that argument is unsupported.
3. **`rumbo2024restore` + `goldani2022outcomes`** — ground the "care concentrated in a handful of centres" finding: Goldani documents that HCPA (which alone accounts for 56/86 CIF-code admissions in the cross-validation) runs the country's most established pediatric MIRP.
4. **`avitzur2024iifr` + `mutanen2023nordic`** — international outcome/etiology benchmarks for the mortality and prematurity/NEC-dominance discussion. Mutanen also guards against over-claiming the "European = congenital enteropathy" contrast (Nordic cohorts are NEC-dominated too).
5. **`khokhar2025global`** (already cited) — for the Discussion, use it for its *explanation* (low LatAm figures = underreporting/infrastructure), not only for its numbers.
